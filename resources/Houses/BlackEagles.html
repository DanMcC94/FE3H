This is the Black Eagle class and my thoughts about them.

Edelgard
Description

Easily one of the strongest characters in the game. She boasts high damage output, strong defenses including resistance, and can fit into pretty much any role. She is resolute with her desire to eliminate the current power structure and bring about a society that is just and doesn't judge based on whether a person posesses a crest or not. The tragedy of her upbringing sowed the seeds for revolution from a very young age. She trusts only a handful of people and only shares information when deemed necessary.

    Bit o' Trivia
  1. Lost items: Eastern Porcelain, Time-worn Quill Pen, White Glove
  2. Her step brother is Dimitri
Strategy

Strengths:

Weaknesses:

Edlegard is a strong leader because she knows what fear is and says bring it on. She is a frontline tank so make sure to get her into roles that nurture that like the armored knight as an early game class. Train her in authority to equip strong batttalions to boost her stats further as well as get gambits to help break shields on monsters. She'll do well with whatever you decide to do, set her up in the enemy danger zone and lead enemies to their doom like flies to honey. Then have the rest of the team collapse on them.

Hubert
Desciption
Hubert is a someone you don't want to be on the opposing side of. He is cunning, cruel, and uncompromising with his ideals. He's a schemer and much like Edelgard he holds his secrets close, but much closer. He doesn't care what others think about him all that matters is that his mission gets done. If you're in his way becareful you don't end up in a ditch. He does have some humanizing moments, but he is subservient to Edelgard to a possible fault depending on how much you like him.

    Bit o' Trivia
  1. Lost items: Folding Razor, Hresvelg Treatise, Noxious Handkerchief
  2. Surprisingly doesn't have a crest presumably because he'd be way too strong otherwise
Strategy

Strengths:

Weaknesses:

Hubert is one of the only characters to learn dark magic and boy does he use it. He is a glass cannon shelling out lots of damage, but while he can dish it out he can't really take it. He makes for a great duelist so have him obliterate enemies that are on their own and keep him deep in the forest so physical hits can't reach him easily. Dip into faith so that he gets some healing skills, but drop it as soon as he gets Recover.

Dorothea
Desciption
Dorothea is a rising star born as a commoner. After her mother died and living on the streets in the Adrestian Empire she found a calling with the Mittlefrank Opera company. It's unclear how long she was destiture for, but she clearly has no intention of ever going back to that life. She feels she needs to find someone to settle down with before her talents and beauty fade.

    Bit o' Trivia
  1. Lost items: Silver Brooch, Songstress Poster, Lovely Comb
  2. Studied under Manuela
Strategy



Dorothea may not have the strongest damage output compared to other mages, however she is very versatile in her ability to deal damage at greater distances without any aid which is highly useful for cleaning up enemy units that can't be reached easily. Keep her in the back dealing damage with spells and healing up units that get low. She gets the spell Thoron which is great for keeping distance and following up even after using a melee and ranged attack. Her ability songstress is actually pretty useful so setup units around her to tank hits and she can recover some of it at the start of your next round. I like to place Dorothea next to Edelgard when I'm baiting enemies in. If you give her enough training in Reason she'll get Meteor which deals damage from long range in an area. You can use this to force enemies to start engaging rather than put someone in danger.

Bernadetta
Desciption

Bernadetta is a timid girl who takes avoidance to a whole new level. She is usually found in her room and rarely makes her way into the sun's rays. She is nervous about how people perceive her and doesn't want to burden others to a fault. Overall she's a sweet and endearing so make sure to visit her door to check in on her.

    Bit o' Trivia
  1. Lost items: Needle and Thread, Still-life Picture, and Hedgehog Case
Strategy

Strengths:

Weaknesses:

Bernadetta is pretty good as an archer. She can deal damage from far away and you can give her a lance for close range combat. She should learn either riding or flying for future class promotion as the extra mobility really helps and being a bow knight gives her extra range. Her crest is great as it acts as a random brave weapon chance and having additional attacks is always great. give her a brave weapon to maximize damage. She learns some pretty good spells overall, but unfortunately her magic growth is abysmal and she'll never be a heavy hitter. It makes more sense to go physical to benefit from her crest and if you do want a bit of magic getting Rescue may be worth it if you go out of your way for it. Not recommended though.

Caspar
Desciption

Brash and headstrong who will rush into danger to defend others. He believes in honor and fighting for his sense of justice. This does lead to him misjudging situations and doing harm with good intentions. Despite this he does his best to learn from his actions even if it takes him time to recognize where he went wrong.

    Bit o' Trivia
  1. Lost items: Thunderbrand Replica, Tattered Overcoat, Grounding Charm
Strategy



Caspar is a very up close and personal character. Axes and gauntlets are his primary weapons and then fill in however you want from there and he'll do well with whatever he's given. He's one of the few guys that doesn't need to worry about learning riding, heavy armor, or flying since he can go into War Master as his final master class. You could give him a bow if you want to give him some range, but he's not particularly strong there or reason so it'll take longer for him to see results, but still worth considering.

Petra
Desciption

A Brigid princess who attends Garreg Mach as a detterent of future war between the Imperials and her home. She is an excellent hunter and warrior who is very prideful of her skills. She deals with a language barrier, but does not let that stop her from learning and is not afraid to ask questions when she feels like she doesn't understand. Her kindness matches her skills as a hunter which is to say she is very caring.

    Bit o' Trivia
  1. Lost items: Exotic Feather, Small Tanned Hide, Annotated Dictionary
Strategy



Hands down one of the strongest characters in the game. She is an absoulute unit who can easily wipe out waves of enemies if set up properly. Her unique ability gives her increased critical chance on enemies with half their health so that seems like a good place to start. Petra is a killer and as such should be given every killer weapon possible. Sword, lance, axe, and bow. She wants them all and to round out her inventory you might as well give her a critical ring to complete the set. If you can give her Alert Stance to boost her evasion and have her wait in forests and thickets the enemies will drop in no time. Keep a healer with Physic on retainer just in case. You do not want to lose her. While she can do this, best to actually put her on the front lines to make your path.

Lindhardt
Desciption

Lindhardt loves the existence of crests and studies them religiously. If he isn't interested in a subject he will usually nap until it goes away. He is generally nice, but is very focused on himself and his wants and desires. He isn't particularly selfish as he does go out of his way from time to time, but he will definitely be thinking about his next nap as he works. He is highly intelligent and incredibly obstinant.

    Bit o' Trivia
  1. Lost items: The Saints Revealed, Feather Pillow, Animated Bait
Strategy

Strengths:

Weaknesses:

Lindhardt is a very good support mage as he gets access to many healing spells as well as having a crest that can improve healing further. He would be the best healer if he had access to Fortify, but even without it he does an excellent job of keeping your army healthy and ready for battle. While he doesn't obtain Fortify he does learn Warp which can send units into enemy backlines or to unlock chests that would otherwise be difficult to reach. His offensive strength is decent overall learning fire and wind spells so he's automatically good against low resistance foes or flying units. Keep him in forests and behind the main line healing up and tearing down.

Ferdinand
Desciption

He's a bit pompous. He has an inferiority complex a mile wide when it comes to Edelgard and it pisses her off. He's a good knight so he really doesn't need to be such a try hard.

    Bit o' Trivia
Strategy

Strengths:

Weaknesses: None

Ferdinand is a defensive unit who can take hits for days when he's geared up. Make sure to train him in axes, lances, and heavy armor. Riding will help to as an end goal and you definitely want to make him into a paladin. I would say go armored knight first so you get some defense first and then exchange some of it for mobility.
