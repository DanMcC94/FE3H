Reason Spells
Rank D

These are the starter spells of the game, each have their own strengths and weaknesses so ultimately it depends on who learns what. Make sure to focus on Reason spells first because unless the unit has fire, you'll need to make every hit count. Work on getting the next level of offensive spells and get Heal as soon as possible as mages need to cycle between healing and damage to scale with the rest of the army.

Rank C

Next level up in terms of power. These spells are stronger than their Rank D counterparts with how much damage they deal, but there is a trade off with the accuracy and weight making them harder to hit as well as making them less likely to double hit. You should decide whether the singular stronger hit is more advantageous over two weaker hits. The only spell here that really stands out is Thoron. There is no ice element for this rank for some reason.

These are the highest level elemental spells for Reason. All boast high might making them highly offensive spells, just don't expect to double with them. Bolting is actually impossible to double with so don't try it. Speaking of which, it's very unfortunate that the best spell of this block is only learned by Hilda and Manuela. A unit who will never use magic, and a unit you'll probably never use. All of these spells are worth it if you can get them.

Unclassified

These spells are the black sheep of the black magic. They don't follow an elemental affinity except Meteor seeming like a fire elemental spell, however, Ragnarok is named the highest level black magic spell so it doesn't belong there. Sagittae and Agnea's Arrow can both be considered arrows as Sagittae is arrow in Latin which is weird because they have standard magic range and don't even come close to Meteor's range. They are all great spells and should be used if a character can use them, but they don't really have a connecting factor. Agnea's Arrow is an upgraded Sagittae, but Sagittae is still strong, and Meteor is a nuke that can be launched across the map and hits adjacent tiles. This can be used to start enemies moving toward you rather than put a unit in danger.
